LRS - local redundant storage

here 3 copies of data will maintained but in same zone.

ZRS - zonal redundant storage

here 3 copies of data maintained but in different zones

Locally Redundant Storage (LRS) replicates data within a single data center, while Zone-Redundant Storage (ZRS) replicates data across three separate availability zones within the same region, offering higher availability and durability at a slightly higher cost.

GRS - geo redundant storage

here there are 2 regions primary and secondary region. 1. here 3 copies of data will maintained with in same region (LRS) 2. then cross region which is secondary region maintain 3 more regions (LRS) where data will be copied async here primary region org will choose secondary will be chosen by azure. usually takes less than 15 min to copy from primary to secondary

Geo-redundant storage (GRS) copies your data synchronously three times to one or more availability zones in the primary region using LRS. It then copies your data asynchronously to a single physical location in a secondary region that is hundreds of miles away from the primary region

GZRS - geo zone redundant storage

here there are 2 regions primary and secondary region. 1.in primary here 3 copies of data will maintained ZRS region (LRS) 2. then cross region which is secondary region maintain 3 more regions (LRS) where data will be copied async here primary region org will choose secondary will be chosen by azure. usually takes less than 15 min to copy from primary to secondary

Geo-redundant storage (GRS) copies your data synchronously three times to one or more availability zones in the primary region using LRS. It then copies your data asynchronously to a single physical location in a secondary region that is hundreds of miles away from the primary region

In Azure, Geo-Zone-Redundant Storage (GZRS) offers the highest level of data protection and availability by replicating data across three availability zones within the primary region and then asynchronously to a single zone in a paired secondary region, ensuring data durability and resilience against both zonal and regional outages
